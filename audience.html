  • Blake Bailey was an ODU star. Faculty and students say he abused and harassed women for years. I led the digital editing for this story, charged with taking a long and quite intense story and optimizing its search potential and reader experience. I worked with the writer to workshop effective subheaders and embed appropriate visual elements for the narrative to complement the investigation.
  • Nearly a dozen children were shot in July in Norfolk. Communities are hurting, and activists want change. I also led the digital editing on this story, which had quite a tight deadline. I compiled the gallery for the lead image, embedded other pictures in a way that helped the story flow and making sure the data visualization added to the reader experience.
  • After years away, ‘Roller Dude’ is back, bringing joy to Chesapeake — and himself. I wrote the headline for this story, changing it from a reporter-written headline: "Chesapeake’s ‘Roller Dude’ is back at his busy intersection, after an absence of years." I tweaked this headline to capture the driving elements of the narrative; namely, that the man's son had died, and he was using his roller skating as a coping mechanism. This story was one of our top stories of the week.
  • Man dies in motorcycle crash that shut down I-64 in Chesapeake. This was a developing story, and with our final update there was a lot of information we needed to convey succinctly, from the details about the man's death to the shutdown of the interstate. I wrote this headline to include the main elements of the brief while distinguishing the story from our many other traffic stories.
  • An honor and a burden: Peninsula native appointed one of youngest bishops with Church of God in Christ. This is another headline that I tweaked from the reporter's suggestion of "Newest bishop with COGIC denomination sees new role as 'an honor' and 'also a burden,'" which was a bit clunky and missed the hard news peg that this was one of the youngest bishops ever appointed in the denomination. I also added the local connection that the bishop was born and raised in Hampton Roads, which we've seen often increases engagement.

Editor in chief and engagement editor at The Diamondback

The DBK's COVID-19 Dashboard

After UMD released its coronavirus dashboard, I kept tabs on the community’s reaction, and I found that many people — staff, faculty, students, parents, College Park residents alike — were struggling to interpret the visualizations. So I used the university’s publicly available data, along with data I got through information act requests and data from the county and state, to provide a more comprehensive and accessible dashboard tracking the extent of the virus’s spread in the area. I updated the data every day for 10 months, tweeting some of the more notable developments.

Live Zoom town hall with UMD’s new president

I worked for weeks to plan, execute and moderate a live Zoom town hall with UMD’s new president about plans for the fall semester, liaising with the university communications department to ensure The Diamondback would have total editorial control over the questions we asked. To promote the event and solicit community questions, I created a newsletter campaign and Twitter and Instagram graphics. Ultimately, 800 students showed up to hear the answers to the questions they cared about.

I also collaborated with different student groups on campus, including the Residence Hall Association and PLUMAS, a Latinx advocacy group, to set up a process for them to ask questions directly to the president. I helped them refine their questions to best reflect the information they wanted to know. For the event itself, I coordinated social media and website coverage, with one reporter live tweeting and one writing an article for our site.
