Live Zoom town hall with UMD’s new president

I worked for weeks to plan, execute and moderate a live Zoom town hall with UMD’s new president about plans for the fall semester, working with the university communications department to ensure The Diamondback would have total editorial control over the questions we asked. To promote the event and solicit community questions, I created a newsletter campaign and Twitter and Instagram graphics. Ultimately, 800 students showed up to hear the answers to the questions they cared about.

I also collaborated with different student groups on campus, including the Residence Hall Association and PLUMAS, a Latinx advocacy group, to set up a process for them to ask questions directly to the president. I helped them refine their questions to best reflect the information they wanted to know. For the event itself, I coordinated social media and website coverage, with a one reporter live tweeting and one reporter writing an article for our site.
